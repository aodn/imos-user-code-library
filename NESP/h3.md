# Slide 1: Introduction to Uber H3
## The World in Hexagons: Introduction to Uber H3
**A Discrete Global Grid System for Geospatial Indexing**

---

### What it is
An open-source, hierarchical hexagonal indexing system developed by Uber to manage and analyze massive spatial datasets.

### The Problem
Traditional Latitude/Longitude pairs are hard to "group" or "join" quickly. Additionally, administrative boundaries (like Marine Regions) are highly detailed not well suited to efficient dataset analysis.

### The Solution
H3 tiles the entire Earth’s surface with hexagons, turning coordinates into unique **64-bit identifiers** for lightning-fast lookups.

---

# Slide 2: Why Hexagons?
## The Geometry of Efficiency

* **Equidistance:** In a square grid, diagonal neighbors are further away than edge neighbors. In a hexagonal grid, all 6 neighbors are the same distance from the center.
* **Reduced Distortion:** Hexagons better approximate the Earth's curvature than squares or triangles.
* **Smoothing:** Hexagons provide a more natural representation for heatmapping.

# Slide 3: Hierarchical Resolution
## Scaling from Continents to Coffee Tables

H3 supports **16 levels of detail**, allowing for seamless transitions between global and local views.

| Resolution | Average Area ($km^2$) | Usage Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0** | ~4.4 Million | Continental Analysis |
| **7** | ~5.16 | Harbour or Bay Analysis |
| **15** | ~0.0000009 | Individual Sample Analysis |

* **Aperture 7:** Each "parent" hexagon contains approximately 7 "child" hexagons.

---

# Slide 4: Key Technical Features
## Built for Performance

* **Fast Joins:** Instead of complex "Point-in-Polygon" math, H3 treats spatial joins as simple integer comparisons.
* **Compactness:** Entire datasets can be "compacted" (using a few large hexagons instead of thousands of small ones) to save storage and reduce lookups.
* **Interoperability:** Written in C with bindings for Python, Java, JavaScript, and more.

# Slide 5: Real-World Applications
## Optimization: Heat Mapping & Shape Tagging

---

### 1. High-Performance Heat Mapping
* **Fixed-Bin Normalization:** Traditional heat maps often use arbitrary radii that cause visual overlap distortion. H3 provides a uniform grid where each cell acts as a precise, non-overlapping bucket.
* **Database-Level Aggregation:** Aggregate millions of points into Hex IDs using simple `GROUP BY` SQL statements. This moves the heavy lifting to the database, making front-end rendering instantaneous.

### 2. Efficient Shape Tagging (Spatial Joins)
* **The "Point-in-Polygon" Killer:** Replaces expensive trigonometric calculations with simple 64-bit integer comparisons.
* **Polyfill Precision:** Represent complex, irregular boundaries (geofences, delivery zones) as a set of H3 indexes.

---

### Key Engineering Benefits
* **Storage:** Store a small set of compression friendly locality sensitive integers instead of complex GeoJSON or WKT strings.
* **Scalability:** Move from $O(N^2)$ spatial intersections to $O(1)$ or $O(N)$ index-based joins.
